Conclusion

Conclusion

The Marvel Cinematic Universe was the first major attempt at a company creating a shared universe of different movies starring different characters all connected by one large overarching storyline. This form of storytelling was so successful that we’ve seen other studios try it with their own franchises but with much less success. Kevin Feige, who has worked as a producer for every single MCU movie, is one of the main reasons for the success since he was originally brought onto the project due to his knowledge of Marvel comics. Warner Bros, for example, tried it unsuccessfully with their series of DC movies receiving mostly a poor reception despite doing well at the box office.

One of the Marvel Cinematic Universe main triumphs was bringing versions of popular characters and storylines to a completely new audience. In the years of the early releases, Marvel franchises like Spider-Man, the X-Men and Fantastic Four were the most popular in terms of both the movies and comics but Phase One helped to elevate the popularity of the Avengers roster. The New Avengers series by Brian Michael Bendis went on to being one of Marvel’s best-selling comic series whereas previously sales lagged below the aforementioned franchises.
