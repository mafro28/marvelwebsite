Phase One

Iron Man (2008)

Directed by John Favreau, Iron Man was the first entry in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) and laid the foundations of what was to come in the next 22 movies. The retelling of Iron Man’s origin story was heavily influenced by Iron Man volume 4 issues 1-6 written by Warren Ellis. It updated and modernised his origin from being captured in the Vietnam War while working as an arms dealer to a more modern-day Afghanistan conflict.

One of the major things introduced in this movie was the introduction of government agent Nick Fury in the post-credits scene. He is played by Samuel L. Jackson here and a version of the character from the comics was actually based on the actors physical appearance so this was rather surreal to see the exact character on screen

The whole point of this scene was to set up forthcoming Thor, Incredible Hulk and Captain America movies that would all lead into an enseble Avengers movie.

Captain America: The First Avenger (2011)

Captain America was the final solo movie of Phase One and chronologically the first in the MCU timeline with it being set during World War 2 in 1943-1945. It details Steve Rogers transformation from weedy army recruit into super human, super soldier Captain America who fought against an offshoot of the Nazis called Hydra led by supervillain Red Skull.

This movie was extremely faithful to the comic incarnation and often had Rodgers fighting alongside the Howling Commandos mercenaries and Bucky, who’s character was changed from a war photographer in the comics to Rodgers’ best friend and squamate.

One of the most surprising moments taken from the comics is when Captain America literally punches Hitler, but in the movie, this takes place as a theatre performance during an inspirational World War 2 propaganda show.

Avengers (2012)

Avengers was a cinematic first in terms of a series of solo films building up to one with an ensemble cast all in the same cinematic universe.

Rather fittingly, it drew some inspiration for the main story from the plot of the very first issue of the Avengers comic released in 1963. This dealt with Loki, God of Mischief, manipulating the Incredible Hulk to go on a rampage and fight the other members of the Avengers.

Another major influence was Scottish writer Mark Millar and artist Bryan Hitch’s Ultimates series. This was part of Marvel’s Ultimate line of comics which were set outside of normal continuity in a different universe and was basically used to relaunch a number of their popular franchises Like Spider-Man, Fantastic Four and X-Men. Ultimates was the Avengers relaunch and was a much more modernised, cynical take on US government-run superhero team set in a post 9/11 world.

The post-credits scene, which would become a staple of the MCU movies for teasing upcoming films, had the first appearance of the villain Thanos who, in Phase Three, would go on to be the main threat in the two final Avengers movies of this 22 film saga.
