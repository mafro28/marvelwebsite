Phase Two

Captain America: The Winter Soldier (2014)

This was the first MCU movie directed by the Russo Brothers who would go on to direct Civil War, Infinity War and Endgame. It was one of the first MCU films that broke from the traditional superhero film mould and often felt like more of a political thriller with themes of espionage. It dealt with the revelation that S.H.I.E.L.D., a secret US government espionage and counter-terrorism division had been secretly infiltrated by the terrorist group Hydra for decades.

Much of the story was heavily influenced by Ed Brubaker’s Captain America volume 5 which introduced the Winter Soldier character who is revealed to be Captain America’s best friend Bucky who was thought to have died in the first movie during World War 2, but was in fact found by Hydra and brainwashed into working as an assassin for decades while being cryogenically frozen in between. This is one of the most accurate versions of a comic character in the entire series of movies with many scenes feeling like straight recreations of the comic panels.

The same can be said for the Falcon who is also introduced and goes on to join the Avengers in future movies, with the inspiration for his costume design taken from the Ultimates comics.

Guardians of the Galaxy (2014)

Guardians was probably the first Marvel movie where there was a potential risk of it bombing at the box office. At the time of its announcement, the characters in question were quite niche and didn't even have their own ongoing comic series. It showed Marvel were willing to take risks and break from the traditional movies they had shot so far.

It was losely based on the 2008 comic run by Dan Abnett and Andy Lanning, with the team consisting of characters from that although Star-Lord's movie personality differs a lot and this in turn ended up influencing the most recent incarnation of him in the relaunched series.

Recommended reading: Guardians of the Galaxy vol. 1: Cosmic Avengers

Avengers: Age of Ultron (2015)

Despite the title, it has nothing to do with the comic event of the same name. It deals with the creation of an artificial intellence by Tony Stark and Bruce Banner for a defence network which ends up becoming sentient and going rogue becuase it believes to save the planet it has to destroy all humanity.

His origin was different than the comic version where Hank Pym, who would later appear in Ant-Man, was Ultron's original creator. An Ant-Man movie directed by Edgar Wright was planned in 2006 well before the MCU concept started but, due to continuiys delays and scheduling conflicts, it wasn't released until 2015 with a new director.

Quicksilver and Scarlet Witch, two classic Avengers from the series' very first run in the 60s, were introduced much like they were back then as villains initially who later changed sides and joined the team. However, unlike the comics, their backstory had to be changed from being mutants and children of Magneto (since Marvel didn't have the rights to mutants or X-Men) to being given powers from an Infinity Stone from HYDRA.

Recommended reading: Avengers: Rage of Ultron
